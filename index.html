Readout

What discovery clarified

Reliability is the center of the project.

Caprock's stated priority is a reliable marketing data system. Dashboards are the useful surface. The work succeeds on trusted data, consistent definitions, and a repeatable process for checking accuracy.

  • Reporting needs to span channels. Paid, organic, web, email, calls, forms, MQLs, geo, campaign, creative, and audience views all need to line up.
  • The new dashboard should feel familiar. The difference is the cleaner data foundation underneath it.
  • Caprock's manual tracker exists because trust is missing. The replacement should make data easy to verify and then display it cleanly.
  • Closed-loop ROI is a later maturity step. Dynamics CRM matters, but the immediate need is to preserve source and campaign data so ROI can be connected cleanly later.

Two buckets of need, on one trusted foundation.

Caprock described two distinct needs: a tool the marketing team can use to analyze performance themselves, and reports for leadership and the board. Both sit on the same foundation of trustworthy, consistently defined data.

  1. Trustworthy data and consistent definitions. Reliable inputs, shared KPIs across channels, and a repeatable way to check accuracy.
  2. A marketing analysis tool. The team can pull and analyze across channels, campaigns, creative, and audiences, with custom date ranges and period-over-period and year-over-year comparisons.
  3. Leadership and board reporting. Summary, channel, and campaign views for the team, plus higher-level summaries built for leadership and board conversations.
  4. Views suited to each audience. Marketing, leadership, board, and eventually regional teams work from the same foundation.

Architecture

The proposed reporting foundation

Every delivery path uses the same foundation: collect the data, normalize it, validate it, then report from that trusted layer. This keeps the new dashboard focused on clear decisions, with sources, definitions, and exceptions resolved in the trusted layer.

1

Collect

Paid channels, organic channels, GA4, CallRail, forms, email, MQLs, and future CRM data.

2

Unify and validate

Shared campaign, channel, geo, date, and KPI definitions with an explicit quality check.

3

Report

New reporting views for marketing, leadership, channel detail, campaign detail, and board-ready summaries.

4

Extend

BigQuery, Squid, Dynamics CRM, and closed-loop ROI can be added as the data model matures.

Open question:

Work chunks

Natural ways to phase value

These are the pieces that could be grouped into one release or sequenced as useful slices. The order should follow what Caprock most wants in scope first.

Tracking and attribution foundation

Enabler

Establish the shared data layer the rest of the work sits on: consistent campaign, source, channel, and KPI definitions, clean form and call capture, reliable GA4 events, and a repeatable accuracy check.

Every downstream readout inherits the quality of this layer, so it comes first.

Paid media performance readout

Fast visibility

Normalize paid media KPIs across Google Ads, LinkedIn, Simpli.fi, YouTube, and related channels, using the consistent metric set Caprock already requested, with breakdowns by campaign, creative, audience, and geography, plus pacing against target.

Leads and MQLs by channel

Executive value

Bring form fills, calls, and Caprock's structured MQL data into the reporting view so leadership and the marketing team can see which channels and regions drive real lead activity, and how leads move through each funnel stage.

Website, SEO, and organic visibility

Context layer

Fold in GA4 website performance, organic search, Google Business Profile, organic social, and related non-paid sources.

Executive and board reporting

Audience layer

Create higher-level summaries for leadership and board conversations, focused on leads, investment, trend, and what needs attention.

Delivery paths

Two reasonable ways to sequence it

Path A: one complete release

Build the system as a full package before Caprock adopts it.

  • Best for minimizing transition churn.
  • More complete at launch.
  • Longer time before the first usable deliverable.

Path B: phased release by priority

Our Recommendation

Pick the highest-value slices first, while keeping the same shared foundation underneath.

  • Best for getting useful reporting into hands sooner.
  • Lets Caprock validate direction on real deliverables as they ship.
  • Requires choosing what to put in scope first.

Why we lean toward phasing.

Phasing puts trustworthy reporting in your hands early, lets Caprock confirm direction on real deliverables, and builds each slice on the same shared foundation so later phases extend the work cleanly. You decide what carries the most value, and we scope from there.

Working assumptions

What we will assume unless corrected

  • We are building a new dashboard experience. The reporting will sit on a cleaner, validated data layer from the start.
  • Daily refresh is the default. Faster refresh is a scoping detail if there is a specific operational need.
  • MQLs can stay manual at first. We will structure that data so it can be included in reporting now and automated later.
  • Dynamics CRM is part of the path. Form and lead attribution should be captured in a way that can flow into CRM and support ROI later.

If any assumption is wrong, tell us now. Otherwise we will use these to prepare the next scope.
